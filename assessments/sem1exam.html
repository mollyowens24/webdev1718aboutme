"In Cold Blood" is a novel that was written by Truman Capote, first published in 1966. It tells the true story of the very well-known Clutter Family murder that occured in Garden City in 1959. The book tells the stores of the Clutter family and the murderers leading up to the event, and follows them throughout their capture, trial, and eventual execution. This book is very unique because it is not a typical murder mystery, because the audience already knows who did the crime. The question it raises is why?

Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur and inventor, most famously known for being the creator of the iPhone. Jobs died in 2011 due to pancreatic cancer, but his company, Apple, is still one of the leading smartphone and electronics producers around the world today.

Famous Chicago Restaurants

Portillos

Portillos is a growing food chain that originated in Chicago in 1963. They are known for their hotdogs, shakes, fries, and chocolate cake. One of the most famous Portillos is in downtown Chicago at Ontario&Clark.

Lou Malnati's Pizza

Lou Malnati's is a pizza restaurant from Chicago. Chicago is known for its deep dish pizza, and many residents of the city will say that Lou Malnati's has the best.

Lou Mitchell's

Lou Mitchell's is another popular restaurant in Chicago, located at Jackson&Clinton near Union Station. They are mainly known for their breakfasts.
